Hippopotamus

A hippo bringing its head out of the water halfway through.

  • Scietific Name: Hippopotamus amphibius.
  • Average length: 10.8 to 17.1 feet.
  • Average Lifespan: 40 to 50 years.
  • Habitat: rivers, lakes and swamps.

The Hippopotamus, scientifically known as Hippopotamus amphibius, is an iconic semi-aquatic mammal native to sub-Saharan Africa with their massive size and barrel-shaped bodies, Hippos spend much of their time submerged in water to keep cool and protect their sensitive skin. Despite their herbivorous diet of grasses and vegetation, they are known for their aggressive nature, especially when defending their territory or young. Despite their formidable appearance, hippos are social animals, often congregating in groups known as pods or bloats. However, habitat loss, hunting, and human-wildlife conflict pose significant threats to Hippopotamus populations across Africa.
